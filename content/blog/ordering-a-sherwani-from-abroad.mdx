We stitch for grooms who never set foot in our Saddar shop until their wedding week — sometimes not even then. A dulha in Dubai, a brother handling the order from London, a groom in Lahore or Karachi who wants Pindi tailoring without the trip. Remote orders work well when they are done properly, and they go wrong in completely predictable ways when they are not. After years of shipping sherwanis out of the city and out of the country, here is exactly how to do it right.

## The order, start to finish

A remote sherwani order is not complicated, but each step matters.

1. **First contact.** You tell us the function, the date, your budget, and send reference photos of looks you like.
2. **Fabric and design.** We recommend fabric for the season, show you options over video or photos, and lock the design and embellishment.
3. **Measurements.** You send accurate naap and clear photos. This is the step everything depends on.
4. **Deposit.** A deposit confirms the order. We do not cut until it clears.
5. **Stitching.** Ten to fifteen working days for a sherwani, longer with heavy handwork or during the Oct–Feb rush.
6. **Video fitting.** Where possible, we do a fitting over video before final finishing.
7. **Balance and shipping.** The balance is paid, then we ship with tracking.

## Measurements are the whole game

Ninety percent of remote-order problems come from bad measurements. The fabric can be perfect and the karigar excellent, but if the naap is wrong, the coat is wrong. So take this part slowly.

- **Get a local tailor or a careful friend to measure you.** Do not guess your own back width and shoulder.
- **Use a proper measuring tape**, not a piece of string and a ruler.
- **Send standing photos** — front, side, and back, against a plain wall, in fitted clothing.
- **Tell us your height and weight.** It lets us sanity-check the numbers against the photos.

Our [sherwani measurements guide](/blog/how-to-take-sherwani-measurements) walks through every measurement we need, and the [measurement guide page](/measurement-guide) has the full checklist you can hand to whoever is helping you. Follow it exactly. A sherwani cut to wrong numbers cannot be fixed by courier.

## Why remote orders are prepaid, not COD

This surprises some first-time buyers, so let us be plain about it. A made-to-measure sherwani is cut to your body. If you change your mind or refuse delivery, we are left with a garment in your exact measurements that no one else can wear. That is why every remote and overseas order is prepaid.

- A **deposit** confirms the order and covers fabric and cutting.
- The **balance** is paid before the parcel ships.
- We send **photos of the finished garment** before shipping so you see it before you pay the balance.

Any tailor offering custom groom wear cash-on-delivery to another country is either inexperienced or about to disappoint someone. Prepaid protects both sides and keeps the karigar committed to your timeline.

## The video fitting

This is the safety net that makes overseas orders reliable. Before we finish the collar and hem, we ask you to try the half-finished sherwani on video — or, if you cannot come in, we fit it on a form matched to your measurements and walk you through it on a call. We are checking:

- Shoulder seam sitting in the right place.
- Sleeve and overall length.
- Waist shaping and chest fit.
- Collar closing clean.

Adjustments are far easier at this stage than after shipping. Build a couple of extra days into your timeline for it.

## Timing for overseas grooms

Shipping adds time, and customs can add more. Plan backwards generously.

| Stage | Allow |
| --- | --- |
| Stitching (sherwani) | 10 to 15 working days |
| Video fitting and adjustments | 2 to 4 days |
| International shipping | 3 to 10 days |
| Customs buffer | a few days |

For a winter wedding in the Oct–Feb season, start the conversation in August or September. The Saddar workrooms fill up fast once the season begins, and an overseas order has the least room for delay. Our note on [how early to order your wedding sherwani](/blog/how-early-to-order-your-wedding-sherwani) covers the full timeline.

## Tips from grooms who got it right

- Place the order earlier than feels necessary. Nobody regrets the extra week.
- Send your measurements once, carefully, rather than three times in a rush.
- Keep one contact person handling the order to avoid mixed messages.
- Confirm the shipping address and your wedding date in writing.

A sherwani made in Saddar and shipped to Dubai or London arrives exactly like one collected in person — provided the naap is right and the timeline is honest.

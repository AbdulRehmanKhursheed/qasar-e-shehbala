If you are getting married in Rawalpindi or Islamabad, your sherwani is one of the few decisions you genuinely cannot rush — and one too many grooms leave to the last two weeks. Here is how to do it properly, from a shop that has been stitching groom wear in Saddar since 1999.

## Start early — this is the whole game

The single best decision you can make is to **start 4 to 6 weeks before your barat**. A made-to-measure sherwani takes 10 to 15 working days of actual work, and that assumes the workshop is not buried.

During *Decemberistan* — the Oct to Feb wedding peak — every good tailor in Pindi and Islamabad is full. Lead times stretch, fittings get rushed, and the relaxed experience you wanted becomes a scramble. If your wedding is in winter, **order in August or September.** You will get more attention, more fittings, and a calmer groom.

## What to actually ask your tailor

Walking into a showroom, most grooms ask the price and the delivery date. Ask these instead:

1. **"Is this made-to-measure or ready-made with alterations?"** There is a huge difference. A true custom sherwani is cut to your body. An altered ready-made piece never sits the same way.
2. **"How many fittings will I get?"** A good answer is at least one mid-process fitting plus a final one. One fitting only is a warning sign on a high-value garment.
3. **"What is the deposit, and what does it cover?"** A 30–50% advance is normal and fair — the fabric is cut for you and cannot be reused. Read our note on deposits below.
4. **"Who does the embroidery, and is it handwork or machine?"** Handwork (dabka, zardozi, tilla) costs more and looks it. Know which you are paying for.
5. **"What happens if the fit is off at the final fitting?"** The answer should be a free adjustment, not an argument.

## The deposit — what is normal

A **30% to 50% advance** is standard for custom groom wear across Pakistan, and it is fair. The moment a karigar cuts your fabric, that cloth is yours — it cannot be sold to anyone else. The deposit protects the tailor against a cancelled order and locks in your slot.

The balance should be due at your **final fitting or on collection**, never fully upfront with no fitting. Be wary of anyone who wants 100% before you have seen the garment on your body. At our shop, we confirm a deposit before cutting and collect the balance when you are happy with the fit.

## A word on COD and out-of-city orders

For high-value groom wear, **cash on delivery is not the right model** — and any serious tailor will tell you the same. A sherwani worth a lakh or more, cut to your exact measurements, cannot simply be returned if you change your mind at the door. That is why deposits exist.

If you are ordering from out of town — Lahore, Karachi, or abroad — you can still get a Rawalpindi sherwani.

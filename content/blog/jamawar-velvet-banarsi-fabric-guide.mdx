The fabric decides almost everything about how your sherwani looks, feels, and photographs — more than the embroidery, more than the colour. Yet most grooms spend ten minutes on it and an hour on the buttons.

Here is how the three fabrics we are asked about most actually compare, from someone who cuts them every week.

## Jamawar

Jamawar is the workhorse of the premium sherwani. It is a woven fabric with a self-pattern running through it — paisleys, florals, geometric motifs — so it looks rich even before any embroidery is added.

- **Feel:** Substantial and structured. It holds a formal shape, which is why a jamawar sherwani looks so composed on stage.
- **Best for:** The barat, especially in winter. It suits almost every build because it does not cling.
- **Watch out for:** A busy jamawar pattern plus heavy embroidery can become too much. If your fabric is already ornate, keep the handwork restrained.

## Velvet

Velvet is the most dramatic of the three. Deep, soft, light-absorbing — a bottle-green or maroon velvet sherwani under indoor lighting is unforgettable.

- **Feel:** Warm and plush. This is its strength in winter and its weakness in summer.
- **Best for:** The Oct–Feb wedding season, indoor functions, and grooms who want a regal, photograph-heavy look. Velvet is having a real moment — see our [groom wear trends for 2026](/blog/groom-wear-trends-2026).
- **Watch out for:** Heat. For a summer barat, velvet will not be comfortable. And cheap velvet looks cheap instantly — this is a fabric where quality is visible from across the room.

## Banarsi

Banarsi brings the shimmer. Woven with fine zari, it carries an ornate, traditional pattern that catches the light beautifully.

- **Feel:** Lighter and softer than jamawar, with more movement.
- **Best for:** Grooms who want something traditional and luminous without the weight of velvet. Lovely for a nikah or a daytime function.
- **Watch out for:** Because it is lighter, it needs good lining and construction to hold a sharp sherwani shape. This is where the karigar matters.

## So which should you choose?

For a December or January barat in Rawalpindi or Islamabad — the heart of *Decemberistan* — it is hard to beat **velvet** for drama or **jamawar** for a more classic, structured look. If your wedding falls outside the cold months, lean toward **banarsi** or a lighter silk-blend and save velvet for another occasion.

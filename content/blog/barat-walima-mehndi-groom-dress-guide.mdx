A Pakistani wedding is not one event — it is three or four, each with its own mood. The groom who plans his looks as a set, rather than panic-buying the week before, always looks better in the photographs. Here is how to dress for each function, and how to do it without buying four outfits you will never wear again.

## Mehndi — colour and comfort

The mehndi is the relaxed one. It is long, it is warm, there is dholki and dancing, and it is the one function where you can genuinely play with colour.

- **What to wear:** A kurta or a light kurta-shalwar in yellow, mustard, deep green, or crisp white, often with light gota or thread detailing.
- **Keep it breathable.** You will be on your feet for hours. Heavy fabric is a mistake here.
- **Add character:** A printed stole, juttis in a contrasting colour, or fresh-flower detailing if the family theme calls for it.

This is the function to enjoy. Save the heavy formality for the barat.

## Barat — the main event

This is the photograph you will frame. Everything we said about getting the barat look right lives in our [sherwani vs prince coat guide](/blog/sherwani-vs-prince-coat), but the short version:

- **Wear the sherwani.** This is its moment — the entrance, the stage, the family photos. Browse the [sherwani collection](/collections/sherwani) for the level of handwork that suits a barat.
- **Finish it properly.** A kulla or pagri, a khussa, and a stole transform a good sherwani into a groom's look.
- **Coordinate with the bride.** Complement her outfit, do not match it. A black or bottle-green sherwani beside a red bridal joora is a timeless pairing.
- **Plan for the day.** Choose a fabric and colour that survives a long, busy day — darker tones hide the inevitable.

## Walima — sharp and semi-formal

The walima is the dinner, the more composed evening. This is where a [prince coat](/collections/prince-coat) earns its place.

- **What to wear:** A well-fitted prince coat in navy, charcoal, or deep green, with a fine [waistcoat](/collections/waistcoats) and a pocket square.
- **Why it works:** It is sharp without being ceremonial, it lets your bride's walima outfit lead, and — crucially — you will wear it again. A good prince coat goes to every friend's wedding for years.

## How to plan three looks without overspending

You do not need to spend equally on all three. Here is the smart split most grooms land on:

1. **Invest in the barat sherwani.** This is the photograph. Put the budget here.
2. **Buy a re-wearable walima prince coat.** It pays for itself across future weddings.
3. **Keep the mehndi light and affordable.** A good kurta does not need to cost much, and you will wear it casually afterward.

| Function | Recommended look | Re-wearable? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mehndi | Kurta / kurta-shalwar | Yes, casually |
| Barat | Embellished sherwani | Rarely — it is *the* outfit |
| Walima | Prince coat + waistcoat | Yes, often |

## Plan it as a set, early

The grooms who look best are the ones who chose all three looks together, weeks ahead, with the colours coordinated and the fabrics chosen for the season. The grooms who struggle are the ones buying the barat sherwani ten days before the wedding.

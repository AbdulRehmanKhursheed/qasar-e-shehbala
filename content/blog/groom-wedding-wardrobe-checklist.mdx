The groom who looks effortless on his wedding days is almost never the one who left it all to the last week. He is the one who planned his wardrobe as a set, started early, and ran it like a small project. A Pakistani wedding is three or four functions, each with its own look, plus accessories and grooming stacked on top. Sixty days is enough time to do it calmly. Here is the countdown we give grooms in our Saddar shop, week by week.

A note before we start: if your wedding falls in the Oct–Feb season, treat this as a minimum and start sooner. The workrooms fill from August onward, and 60 days in peak season is tighter than it sounds.

## Day 60 to 50 — decide and order

This is the most important fortnight. Everything downstream depends on getting the orders placed now.

- Decide your looks for **all** functions: mehndi, barat, and walima. Plan them together, not one at a time.
- Choose fabrics for the season — read our [fabric guide](/blog/jamawar-velvet-banarsi-fabric-guide) so you know jamawar from banarsi before you walk in.
- Place your main orders. The barat [sherwani](/collections/sherwani) takes the longest, so it goes first.
- Take measurements properly and lock them. The whole project rests on accurate naap.
- Pay your deposits and get receipts with delivery dates written on them.

If you are still torn between garments, our [barat, walima, and mehndi guide](/blog/barat-walima-mehndi-groom-dress-guide) settles most of it.

## Day 50 to 40 — confirm the supporting pieces

The big garments are in the workroom. Now sort everything around them.

- Order the [waistcoat](/collections/waistcoats) for the walima or nikah if it is separate from the coat.
- Choose your mehndi kurta — light fabric, light work, comfortable for dancing.
- Confirm colours with the bride for each function. Complement, do not match.
- Start shortlisting accessories: kulla or pagri, khussa, stole, brooch, pocket square.

## Day 40 to 30 — first fitting

Your first fitting falls in here. This is where the cut gets checked against your body before the finishing work goes in.

- Attend the **first fitting** for the barat sherwani. Watch the shoulder, the length, and the collar.
- Flag anything that feels off now, not later. Early alterations are cheap and easy.
- Buy or confirm your footwear — khussa and formal shoes both, depending on your functions.
- Book your grooming appointments: barber, facial, any skin or hair work you want done before the photos.

## Day 30 to 20 — second fitting and accessories

- Attend the **second fitting** as the embellishment goes on. The garment should be close to final shape now.
- Finalise every accessory. Lay them out function by function so nothing is missing.
- Try the full barat look together — sherwani, kulla, stole, khussa — and look in a proper mirror.
- If you ordered from out of city, this is when your video fitting and shipping should be happening. See our [out-of-city ordering guide](/blog/ordering-a-sherwani-from-abroad).

## Day 20 to 10 — final fitting

The home stretch. Around 7 to 10 days out, do the final fitting on the body you will actually have on the day.

- **Final fitting** for all main garments. Confirm fit, length, and finishing.
- Pay balances and collect receipts.
- Do your last grooming round — haircut and beard shaping a few days before, not the morning of.
- Pack a small wedding-day kit: spare buttons, a safety pin or two, lint roller, the right inner shirt.

## Day 10 to 0 — collect and rest

- **Collect every garment** and try each one on at home. Do not assume; check.
- Hang everything by function so nothing gets confused in the morning rush.
- Steam or press as needed — a wrinkled sherwani undoes all the work.
- Brief whoever is helping you dress, especially with the kulla and stole.
- Rest. A tired dulha photographs tired.

## The at-a-glance timeline

| Days out | Main task |
| --- | --- |
| 60 to 50 | Decide all looks, place orders, lock naap |
| 50 to 40 | Order waistcoat and mehndi kurta, confirm colours |
| 40 to 30 | First fitting, footwear, grooming booked |
| 30 to 20 | Second fitting, finalise accessories |
| 20 to 10 | Final fitting, pay balances, last grooming |
| 10 to 0 | Collect, try on, steam, rest |

## The one rule above all

Order early and plan the looks together. Almost every wardrobe disaster we see comes from the same root — a groom who decided one function at a time and started too late. Sixty days, run properly, is comfortable. Thirty days in peak season is a gamble.

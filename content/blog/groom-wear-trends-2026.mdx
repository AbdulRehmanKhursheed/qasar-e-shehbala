Every wedding season we see what hundreds of grooms gravitate toward, and 2026 has a clear personality: **confident restraint**. Less shine, more depth. Fewer loud golds, more considered detail. Here is what is actually being stitched and worn across Rawalpindi and Islamabad this year.

## 1. Black is the colour of the year

The black sherwani has gone from a bold choice to the default for the fashion-forward groom. It is dramatic, it photographs with incredible contrast against a red or maroon bridal outfit, and it hides the chaos of a long barat day. Paired with tonal black embroidery and a black kulla, it is the strongest look in the room.

## 2. Deep jewel tones for everyone else

For grooms who want colour, 2026 is all about depth rather than brightness:

- **Bottle green** — the standout of the season, especially in velvet.
- **Oxblood maroon** — rich and traditional without being predictable.
- **Midnight navy** — versatile enough to cross from barat to walima.

If you are choosing a fabric for these colours, our [fabric guide](/blog/jamawar-velvet-banarsi-fabric-guide) covers how velvet and jamawar carry deep tones differently.

## 3. Tonal embroidery over all-over gold

This is the biggest shift. The fully-encrusted gold sherwani has cooled off. In its place: **tonal work** — matching thread on matching fabric, so the texture catches the light without screaming for attention. Handwork is concentrated where it counts — the collar, the placket, the cuffs — and the rest of the garment is left clean.

It is a more expensive-looking, more grown-up aesthetic. And it ages far better in photographs.

## 4. The minimalist prince coat owns the walima

For the second function, grooms are reaching for a sharp [prince coat](/collections/prince-coat) in navy, charcoal, or deep green — minimal embellishment, excellent fit, worn with a fine waistcoat and a pocket square. It is semi-formal, genuinely re-wearable, and photographs clean. If you are deciding between the two garments, here is our full [sherwani vs prince coat breakdown](/blog/sherwani-vs-prince-coat).

## 5. Accessories are doing more work

The garment is only part of the look this year:

- **Kulla and pagri** in contrasting or tonal cloth, often with a brooch.
- **Khussa** in deep colours rather than plain gold.
- **Stoles and shawls** thrown over the shoulder for the barat entrance.
- **Statement buttons** — antique gold, enamel, or fabric-covered.

A well-chosen [waistcoat](/collections/waistcoats) under an open sherwani or prince coat is also having a strong year.

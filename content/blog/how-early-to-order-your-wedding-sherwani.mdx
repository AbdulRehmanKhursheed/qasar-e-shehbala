The single most common regret we hear in our Saddar shop is not about colour or fabric. It is "kaash thora pehle aa jata". The groom came too late, the karigars were swamped, and we had to either rush the work or simplify it. So let me answer the question every dulha should ask first: how early should you order? After running this shop since 1999, here is the realistic answer, not a comforting one.

## The short answer

For a normal function with proper handwork and real fittings, **four to six weeks before the event** is the comfortable window. During peak season, **six to eight weeks** is safer. Anything under three weeks is a rush, and under two weeks we start saying no to heavy work.

## Why a sherwani is not a quick job

A wedding sherwani is not one task. It is a chain of them, and each link needs time.

1. **Consultation and naap.** Choosing fabric, style, embroidery and taking measurements. One sitting.
2. **Cutting and base construction.** The panels, canvas chest and lining. A few days.
3. **Embroidery.** This is the long pole. Hand dabka, tilla or zardozi on a collar and placket can take a karigar several days to two weeks depending on density.
4. **First and second fittings.** We adjust the chest, shoulders and length on your body, not on a dummy.
5. **Final finishing.** Buttons, lining close-up, pressing, accessories.

Skip or compress any of these and the result shows. The fittings are usually the first casualty of a late order, and fittings are exactly what separate a sharp sherwani from a sack.

## The season rush is real

If you are getting married between October and February, understand that you are competing for the same karigars as every other dulha in Rawalpindi and Islamabad. We call peak wedding season Decemberistan for a reason. The phone does not stop.

Here is roughly how the calendar looks from our side of the counter:

| Period | What is happening | What it means for you |
| --- | --- | --- |
| March to July | Off-peak, karigars available | Easiest time to order, most attention to detail |
| August to September | Booking ramp begins | Good time to lock your slot for a winter wedding |
| October to February | Peak Decemberistan | Workshops full, longer lead times, book six to eight weeks ahead |

The lesson is simple. A summer or monsoon wedding gives you breathing room. A December barat means you should be talking to us by September at the latest.

## What lengthens the timeline

A few things make a sherwani take longer, and you should factor them in:

- **Heavy embroidery.** Dense zardozi adds days, sometimes a week or more, over a lightly worked piece.
- **Fabric sourcing.** If you want a specific imported velvet or a particular banarsi that we do not have in stock, ordering it adds time. Our [fabric guide](/blog/jamawar-velvet-banarsi-fabric-guide) helps you decide early so we are not waiting on cloth.
- **A complicated fit.** Bodies that need more balancing take an extra fitting.
- **You changing your mind.** Switching the colour or design mid-stitch resets parts of the work. Decide and commit.

For the full breakdown of where each working day goes, see our piece on [sherwani stitching time in Pakistan](/blog/sherwani-stitching-time-in-pakistan).

## What happens when you are late

We are not going to pretend rushes are impossible. They are not. But here is the honest trade:

- We may apply a rush charge, because a karigar has to work nights or set aside other orders.
- We will steer you toward lighter, faster handwork, because dense zardozi cannot be hurried without it looking hurried.
- You will likely get one fitting instead of two or three.
- Fabric choice narrows to what we already have.

A rushed sherwani can still look good. It will rarely look as good as the same money spent with four extra weeks. If your date is tight, do not waste a day debating, just come in and let us see what is genuinely possible.

## A simple planning rule

If you are the kind of person who likes one rule to remember: **count back six weeks from your function and put a reminder there. For a winter wedding, count back eight.** That is your "talk to the tailor" date, not your "panic" date.

And do not forget that your sherwani is only one piece of the day. You will also need the right inner shirt, shoes, kulla or dupatta, and a separate outfit for walima or mehndi if you are dressing differently. Plan the whole wardrobe at once.
